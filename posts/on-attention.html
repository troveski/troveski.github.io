On Attention

June 2026

The ancients worried about how to live. We worry about where to look. It sounds like a smaller problem, but I'm no longer sure it is. Attention is the part of us that actually touches the world. Whatever it lands on becomes, for that moment, our entire life.

Most of what competes for it now was engineered to win. A field of crops doesn't try to be more interesting than the field next to it. A feed does. So the contest is no longer fair, and losing it quietly costs us the only thing we can't earn back.

I don't think the answer is to retreat from all of it. The answer is to choose, on purpose, what gets to occupy us — and to notice when something is occupying us that we never chose. That noticing is most of the work. The rest is just being willing to look away.

This is the first of these. I don't know yet what the others will be about. That's part of why I'm writing them.
